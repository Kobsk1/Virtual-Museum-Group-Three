BIOGRAPHY

Napoleon "Billy" Veloso Abueva

(January 26, 1930 - February 16, 2018)

Napoleon Veloso Abueva was born on January 26, 1930, in Tagbilaran City, Bohol. He is known as the Father of Modern Philippine Sculpture for his creative and modern style in making art. He studied Fine Arts at the University of the Philippines under Guillermo Tolentino.

During World War II, Abueva lost his parents, which deeply affected him and inspired his art. Abueva used many different materials in his sculptures, such as wood, metal, and stone. Some of his famous works are Kaganapan, The Transfiguration, and Nine Muses.

In 1976, he was named National Artist for Sculpture, the youngest person to receive the award. He also became the Dean of the University of the Philippines College of Fine Arts. Napoleon Abueva passed away on February 16, 2018, but his artworks continue to inspire many Filipinos today.
